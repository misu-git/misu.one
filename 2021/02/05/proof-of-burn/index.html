Proof of Burn Consensus

The Proof of Burn (PoB) concept

Proof of burn is one of the various consensus mechanism methods used by a blockchain network to ensure that all participating nodes agree on the blockchain network’s real and valid state.

This algorithm is in place to prevent any cryptocurrency coin from being double-spent.

Proof of burn is based on the idea of miners “burning” the coins that provide them mining rights.

The blockchain is a cryptocurrency’s core database.

It stores all transaction-related information in blocks, which serve as the blockchain’s data storage units.

Only when all of the blockchain nodes agree on a set of transactions that they consider valid, a block is recorded.

Because of the blockchain network’s independent and decentralized character, an automated system is essential to ensure that all participating nodes agree on only acceptable transactions.

Consensus-mechanism algorithms handle this critical duty.

What is Proof of Burn?

Proof of burn (POB) is an alternative consensus algorithm that tries to address the high energy consumption issue of a POW system.

POB is often called a POW system without energy waste. It operates on the principle of allowing miners to “burn” virtual currency tokens. They are then granted the right to write blocks in proportion to the coins burnt.

Iain Stewart, the creator of the POB algorithm, compares burned coins to mining rigs to describe the method.

In this example, a miner uses his or her money to purchase a virtual mining setup that allows them to mine blocks.

The miner’s virtual mining “rig” will grow in size as more coins are burned.

You can use the network to send transactions that will burn your own cryptocurrency tokens.

Other participants can mine/burn on top of your block, and you can add other participants’ transactions to your block as well.

Essentially, all of this coin-burning activity maintains the network nimble, and players are rewarded for their efforts (both their own and others’ coins).
