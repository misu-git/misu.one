NFTs with blockchain domains are the newest addition to the blockchain party.
These intriguing assets, which are part URL and part collectible, are quickly becoming the hottest new crypto in town.

Before we get started, you should understand how domain names function.

Generics of Domain Names

As I covered in the Namecoin Post, computers connected to the internet have an IP address. These unique addresses allow individual devices to be identified on networks.

However, they’re long and hard to remember. For example, misu.one’s IP address is 185.199.108.153. Not particularly memorable, is it?

Domain names are a more convenient option.

In your browser’s address field, instead of typing “185.199.108.153”, type “misu.ine” and press enter.

Your browser detects that the website “misu.one” is hosted at the aforementioned IP address automatically.

Non-fungible Tokens in the Blockchain Domain

Non-fungible tokens are a new addition to the blockchain realm (NFTs).

They combine the convenience of NFT trading with the customizability of blockchain-based domain names.

They’ve also experienced a surge in consumer and investment interest in recent months, for a variety of reasons.

Users can buy blockchain domains and even entire domain namespaces via companies like Unstoppable Domains and protocols like Namecoin.

Users can later rent or sell specific domain NFTs through the latter.

Individual “name.crypto” domains can be rented or sold by the owner of “.crypto” domains on a specific blockchain protocol.

This could be a once-in-a-lifetime chance for early investors.
