The Coinbase Effect

Investigating the “Coinbase Effect”

Analyzing the impact of digital token listings on one of the most well-known cryptocurrency exchanges

When new listings are announced, this well-known phenomenon in the cryptoworld investigates and quantifies the positive effects on digital asset values.

Let’s start from the beginning for those who are unfamiliar with the crypto realm.

Coinbase is the largest cryptocurrency exchange in the United States, and many people consider it to be the best way to get started with cryptocurrencies.

Its prospects have improved even more as a result of the boom over the last 12 months.

So much so that on April 14, the crypto exchange became public under the ticker ‘COIN.’

The ‘Coinbase effect’ is explained as follows: When a cryptocurrency is listed on a popular exchange, it immediately obtains exposure to a new set of market players and increases in value.

The effect has previously been thoroughly researched, with Coinbase serving as the primary exchange.

However, this study expands on past research by include five more crypto exchanges to examine what kind of influence identical listings on other platforms have.

Data from six centralized cryptocurrencies are listed below:

1. Coinbase(28 listings)

2. Binance (22 listings)

3. FTX (19 listings)

4. Gemini (19 listings)

5. Kraken (11 listings)

6. OKEx (14 listings)

To capture the effect, the researchers looked at the distribution of cumulative returns five days following the token listing as a preliminary estimate.

Coinbase listings, as expected, provided the highest average return of 91 percent, but they also had the biggest distribution, with returns ranging from -32 percent to 645 percent.

When compared to other exchanges, Coinbase listings result in larger returns, as evidenced by the data above.

The Coinbase dataset did, however, contain some outliers, as shown in the second figure on the right, which highlights six listings that saw outsized rises.

The following were among them:

  • District0x — (645%)
  • Civic — (492%)
  • Band protocol — (234%)
  • The Graph — (169%)
  • Numeraire — (167%)
  • Skale network — (135%)

According to the data, exchange listings, particularly Coinbase, almost always result in an increase in asset returns.
However, the overall market situation at the time of listing is a crucial component to consider when tabulating such findings.
